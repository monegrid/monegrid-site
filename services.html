Facility line · behind the meter

Short-circuit, coordination & arc-flash studies

OSHA and NFPA 70E require an arc-flash risk assessment, reviewed whenever the system changes and at least every 5 years, per IEEE 1584. NEC 110.24 requires available-fault-current labeling. This is recurring, mandated work for data centers, industrial plants, hospitals, and campuses — and we run it in SKM PTW / ETAP.

The compliance drivers

None of this work is optional. Four regulatory drivers set the demand — and the deadlines.

NERC PRC-029-1

Inverter-based resources must prove fault ride-through — by Oct 1, 2026 (bulk electric system) and Jan 1, 2027 (non-BES).

FERC Order 2023

Validated EMT models are required wherever an EMT study is run — no model, no study, no interconnection.

NFPA 70E + IEEE 1584

Arc-flash risk assessments must be reviewed when the system changes and at least every 5 years — recurring by design.

NEC 110.24

Equipment must be labeled with available fault current — which means a current short-circuit study behind every label.
