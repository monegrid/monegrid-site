How it works Under the hood

Faults move faster than reactive monitoring

Microgrids run on low inertia and a growing mix of inverters, batteries, and renewables. Disturbances propagate in sub-second timescales, and threshold alarms only fire after something has already gone wrong.

Low inertia, fast dynamics

Inverter-dominated grids lack the rotational mass that slows down disturbances. Frequency and voltage can swing within cycles.

Complex fault signatures

Distributed energy resources change how faults look. A single fixed threshold can't separate a real fault from normal operation.

Reactive by design

Conventional protection trips on a fault that is already happening. There is no early warning and no localization of the root cause.

Under the hood

SIGMA is built as a pipeline: simulate or stream the signals, learn what normal looks like, score deviations, and route alerts.

RTDS / Pandapower / sensors → feature extraction → anomaly model → alert + localization

Data & simulation

Fault and disturbance scenarios are generated in RTDS and Pandapower, giving labeled data across conditions that are rare or unsafe to reproduce on a live system.

Signal processing

Time-series features are extracted from electrical and thermal channels — capturing the transient behavior that distinguishes a developing fault from normal load variation.

Anomaly model

A machine-learning model learns the system's normal operating envelope and scores deviations in real time, classifying fault signatures rather than tripping on a single threshold.

Integration

Output is delivered as alerts into existing SCADA and monitoring dashboards. The whole system runs in software, alongside infrastructure that is already in place.
